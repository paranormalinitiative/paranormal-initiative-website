Audio Recorders

Audio recorders preserve sound for later review. They are essential for interviews, room tone, EVP sessions, event markers, and contamination checks.

Equipment & Technology EVP Basics

Guide

What They Capture

A recorder captures sound pressure in the environment. It records voices, footsteps, clothing noise, traffic, HVAC, plumbing, device handling, insects, and room resonance. It does not know which sounds matter.

Session Discipline

Log recorder placement, microphone direction, gain, file name, start time, people present, and every known sound during the session. Spoken markers such as "chair moved" or "car passing" protect the review later.

Review Caution

A voice-like sound is a candidate, not a conclusion. Preserve the original file, review copies separately, check contamination, and avoid telling listeners what to hear before independent review.
