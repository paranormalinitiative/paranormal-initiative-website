Lesson Reading

Why ACS Exists

ACS was built to support EVP and ITC experimentation in a more disciplined workspace. Instead of treating random words or radio fragments as answers, ACS emphasizes session structure, phoneme and allophone material, documentation, and review.

The Phoneme Idea

Phonemes and allophones are speech-level building blocks. They are not predefined sentences and they are not a fixed answer list. That makes them useful for experimentation because any perceived structure can be reviewed, compared, documented, and evaluated for possible or plausible communication.

A Research Tool For Careful Interpretation

ACS helps create, capture, organize, and review experimental material for spirit communication, EVP, and ITC study. It does not make conclusions automatically. The strongest interpretation comes from the researcher, the documentation, the session context, and the strength of the surviving evidence.
