Lesson Reading

Why ACS Exists

ACS was built to support EVP and ITC experimentation in a more disciplined workspace. Instead of treating random words or radio fragments as answers, ACS emphasizes session structure, phoneme and allophone material, documentation, and review.

The Phoneme Idea

Phonemes and allophones are speech-level building blocks. They are not predefined sentences and they are not a fixed answer list. That makes them useful for experimentation because any perceived structure must still be reviewed, compared, documented, and treated as uncertain.

A Research Tool, Not A Verdict Machine

ACS does not determine whether something is paranormal. It helps create, capture, organize, and review experimental material. The conclusion still belongs to the researcher, the documentation, and the limits of the evidence.
