Lesson Reading

Why Visual ITC Is Difficult

Water, steam, smoke, mirrors, and reflective surfaces are naturally pattern-rich. They can produce faces, figures, letters, and forms through light, motion, and expectation. This makes them interesting for research and easy to overstate.

A Better Visual Session

Use a fixed camera when possible. Record lighting, surface, container, background, distance, frame rate, and environmental changes. Keep unedited video and still frames. Compare candidates against baseline footage and adjacent frames.

Connection To The Site

The Water ITC and Steam ITC pages can become focused experiment guides later. For now, this lesson sets the standard: visual ITC is experimental, not guaranteed communication, and every result needs context.
