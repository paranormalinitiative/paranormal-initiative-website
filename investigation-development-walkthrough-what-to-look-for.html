Lesson 9: What You Should Be Looking For During A Walkthrough

A walkthrough is only as effective as the investigator conducting it. Without focus, it becomes casual observation, and casual observation has no place in a structured investigation.

Series Index Walkthrough Lesson

A Professional Walkthrough Is Intentional

A professional walkthrough is intentional, structured, analytical, and disciplined.

You are not simply walking through a space. You are identifying variables, establishing context, and actively searching for sources of misinterpretation before the investigation ever begins.

This is one of the most critical stages of any case because what you fail to recognize during the walkthrough, you may misinterpret during the investigation.

Shift The Mindset

During a proper walkthrough, your mindset must shift.

You are not looking for paranormal activity. You are looking for explanations.

This includes identifying structural, environmental, and external influences that can mimic or create the illusion of paranormal phenomena.

Structural And Mechanical Sources

Loose floorboards, shifting materials, and structural settling can produce sounds that resemble footsteps or movement.

HVAC systems and ductwork can create airflow, pressure changes, and noises that may be mistaken for voices, breathing, or movement.

Water pipes and plumbing systems can produce knocking, tapping, vibrations, and pressure changes that may feel like intelligent interaction if they are not identified early.

Electrical And Visual Variables

Electrical panels, wiring, and power fluctuations can influence equipment readings, especially EMF-based tools.

Reflective surfaces such as glass, mirrors, polished furniture, and electronics can create visual anomalies and shadow movement.

External light sources, including passing vehicles, streetlights, and neighboring properties, can cast moving shadows that appear intentional when the environment is dark.

Environmental Activity

Animals, insects, and ordinary environmental activity can create both sound and visual disturbances that are easily misidentified in low-light conditions.

These causes are not rare. They are among the most common sources of reported paranormal activity.

A professional investigator does not dismiss these factors. They document them, understand them, and account for them.

Why The Reference Point Matters

Once the investigation begins, the environment changes. Lighting changes. Sound perception changes. Awareness increases. Expectation rises.

What was once explainable can quickly feel unexplained.

The walkthrough gives investigators a baseline of understanding. It allows them to say, "I have heard that sound before," "I have seen that shadow before," or "I know what caused that."

The First Investigation

Without that reference point, everything becomes questionable. And when everything is questionable, everything becomes vulnerable to misinterpretation.

This is why the walkthrough is not a formality. It is your first investigation.

A proper walkthrough is not about confirming a claim. It is about challenging it.

The Question That Protects The Case

A professional investigator does not begin by asking, "Is this paranormal?"

They begin by asking, "What else could this be?"

That question protects the integrity of the entire investigation.
