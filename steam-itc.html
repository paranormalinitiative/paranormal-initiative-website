EVP / ITC Research

Steam ITC studies vapor movement, light interaction, and camera capture as an experimental visual field.

Steam and vapor can create complex forms that change rapidly. The research value is in disciplined capture, careful comparison, and honest documentation, not in assuming that a shape or face-like pattern is paranormal.

01

Environment Notes

Record air movement, humidity, temperature, light source, background contrast, steam source, distance, camera settings, and room activity.

02

Baseline Recording

Capture vapor movement without prompts to establish normal motion, turbulence, reflections, and camera exposure behavior.

03

Experiment Window

Use timed prompts, consistent placement, and verbal markers while avoiding suggestive commentary during capture.

04

Frame Review

Review motion before and after any interesting frame, identify light and airflow causes, and keep original footage untouched.

Research Caution

Steam ITC is experimental. Vapor, light, shadows, compression, and human pattern recognition can produce striking imagery. The responsible conclusion is often "interesting, unresolved, and worth documenting."
