Basic Field Safety and Permission

An ethics and methodology paper on access, risk, property respect, and investigator responsibility.

Ethics and Professional Standards Investigation Science

Abstract

Safety and permission are not side issues in paranormal investigation. They are foundational requirements. No evidence is worth injury, trespassing, property damage, or disrespecting people and places.

This paper outlines basic field standards for access, safety, and responsible conduct.

Permission First

Investigators must have permission before entering private property, restricted sites, abandoned structures, cemeteries with access rules, businesses, historical properties, or controlled public locations.

Permission should be documented whenever possible. Verbal permission may be acceptable in some contexts, but written permission provides clarity and protection.

Safety Assessment

Before field work, evaluate structural hazards, unstable floors, low visibility, weather, wildlife, traffic, water, mold, sharp debris, electrical hazards, and emergency access.

Safety planning includes communication, lighting, first-aid supplies, charged phones, location sharing, and knowing when to leave.

Respect for Place

Investigators should leave locations as they found them. Do not remove objects, disturb graves, force entry, damage structures, or treat historical places as props.

Respect for property and history is part of professional credibility.

Conclusion

Field safety and permission define the investigator's character. Responsible investigation begins with respect for people, places, law, and the wellbeing of the team.
