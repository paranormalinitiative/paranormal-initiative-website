EMF & K2 Meters

EMF and K2-style meters can help document electrical field conditions, but a spike is not a conversation or a haunting verdict.

Equipment & Technology Terminology Reference

Guide

What They Measure

EMF meters measure electromagnetic fields produced by electrical current, wiring, devices, appliances, power systems, and nearby electronics. K2-style meters make those field changes visible through simple lights.

Common Triggers

Wall wiring, outlets, breaker panels, phones, radios, battery condition, routers, appliances, power lines, nearby equipment, and investigator movement can all create readings or light responses.

How To Use Them Carefully

Establish baselines, map walls and known electronics, repeat the reading, move the device, compare with another meter if available, and log the exact time and location before interpreting the response.
