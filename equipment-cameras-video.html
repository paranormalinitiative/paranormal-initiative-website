Cameras & Video

Cameras document light, scene layout, investigator movement, visual conditions, and possible visual candidates. They are valuable when the original files and scene context are preserved.

Equipment & Technology Evidence Compendium

Guide

What They Record

Still cameras and video systems record light reaching a sensor. Night vision and full-spectrum setups add additional light behavior and extra review concerns. The camera records an image; the investigator must explain how the image may have formed.

Common Artifacts

Dust, insects, lens flare, reflections, breath vapor, compression artifacts, motion blur, rolling shutter, low-light noise, infrared bloom, and camera straps or fingers can all look strange in photos or video.

Good Practice

Save originals, note camera position and lighting, compare adjacent frames, document who was in the room, and test suspected reflection or shadow paths before treating a visual as unresolved.
