Delaware

4 Locations

Delaware's paranormal profile features colonial-era religious sites, Revolutionary War landmarks, Civil War military history, riverfront mansions, and long-occupied hospitality properties.

Fort Delaware

Military Haunted Historic
Delaware City, DE
Civil War Fort
Access: Public / Ferry Access

Union fortress and Civil War prison on Pea Patch Island.

Activity Reported
  • Apparition reports associated with Civil War soldier and prisoner areas
  • Footsteps and movement sounds in casemate and corridor zones
  • Voices and call-outs reported in confinement-related areas
  • Cold spots and atmospheric heaviness in low-light interior sectors
  • Residual military and prison folklore tied to specific rooms and passageways
Structure Layout
  • Main fort interior and parade-ground access
  • Casemates, passage corridors, and confinement areas
  • Barracks and support rooms
  • Exterior ramparts and island-facing perimeter zones
  • Ferry-linked public entry sequence from Delaware City
Location Data

Address: 45 Clinton St, Delaware City, DE 19706

Website: https://www.destateparks.com/park/fort-delaware/

Coordinates: 39.5932° N, 75.5719° W

Classification

Residual military and confinement imprint. Island exposure, ferry transport, and fort acoustics require careful baseline control before elevating auditory reports.

Rockwood Park & Museum

Museum Haunted Historic
Wilmington, DE
Historic Mansion
Access: Public

1850s Gothic Revival estate, one of Delaware's most investigated sites.

Activity Reported
  • Apparition and presence reports tied to upper rooms and hallways
  • Footsteps in staircase and landing areas during quiet periods
  • Cold spots in restored historic rooms
  • Object-movement and subtle room-disturbance folklore
  • Visual anomaly reports from staff, visitors, and event guests
Structure Layout
  • Gothic Revival mansion interior
  • Main stair hall and upper-room circulation
  • Formal rooms and museum exhibit spaces
  • Grounds, gardens, and estate approach areas
  • Mixed museum and park-use environment
Location Data

Address: 4651 Washington Street Extension, Wilmington, DE 19809

Website: https://www.newcastlede.gov/431/Rockwood-Park-Museum

Coordinates: 39.7725° N, 75.5210° W

Classification

Possible intelligent residential haunting in a preserved museum setting. Stair hall and upper rooms remain the strongest comparative zones for repeated-session work.

  • Visual anomaly reports associated with low-traffic periods
  • Government-residence lore amplified by long-term public fascination
Structure Layout
  • Historic governor’s residence and formal reception rooms
  • Central hall and stair circulation
  • Private upper residential areas
  • Grounds and approach from Kings Highway
  • Restricted-access government-use environment

Old Swedes Church

Church Haunted Historic
Wilmington, DE
1698 Church
Access: Public / Tours

One of the oldest churches in the U.S. with colonial burial ground.

Activity Reported
  • Presence sensations in church interior and burial-ground approach areas
  • Footsteps and subtle movement reports during quiet visitation periods
  • Atmospheric changes near older grave-marker sections
  • Auditory anomaly folklore tied to the historic sanctuary
  • Low-light visual disturbance reports within the churchyard
Structure Layout
  • Historic church sanctuary
  • Churchyard and colonial burial-ground perimeter
  • Entry paths and adjacent heritage-site structures
  • Mixed interior religious and exterior cemetery context
  • Public tour and historic-site circulation zones
Location Data

Address: 606 N Church St, Wilmington, DE 19801

Website: https://oldswedes.org/

Coordinates: 39.7386° N, 75.5413° W

Residual religious and burial-ground imprint. The churchyard and sanctuary are best approached with quiet, documentation-first methodology and strong respect-site protocols.
