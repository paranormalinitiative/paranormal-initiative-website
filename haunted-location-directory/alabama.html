Alabama contains one of the highest concentrations of documented paranormal activity in the continental United States, with industrial, antebellum, military, and civic-era sites all represented across the state.

Sloss Furnaces

Industrial Haunted Historic
Birmingham, AL
National Historic Landmark
Access: Public

Former steel production facility operating 1882–1971. Site of numerous worker deaths during operation. One of the most documented industrial hauntings in the United States.

Activity Reported
  • Shadow figures near furnace stacks
  • Unexplained loud bangs and metallic impacts
  • Disembodied voices in the blower building
  • Temperature anomalies near casting areas
  • Apparitions of former workers in period clothing
  • High EMF spikes throughout the facility
Structure Layout

  • Upper Catwalks
  • Blower Building

  • Slag Pit Area
Location Data

Classification

Residual Intelligent Haunting — Multiple distinct entities identified. High EMF baseline anomalies present throughout facility. Considered one of the most active industrial haunting sites in the southeastern United States.

Old Cahawba (Cahawba Archaeological Park)

Ghost Town Haunted Historic
Cahaba, AL (Dallas County)

Alabama's first state capital (1820–1826), abandoned after repeated flooding and now a preserved ghost town. The former city contains ruins of a church, antebellum homes, a plantation cemetery, and is widely regarded as one of the most haunted sites in the state.

  • Mysterious lights (the "Cahawba Lights" phenomenon)

  • Shadow figures near former building foundations
  • Cold spots along the river-adjacent ruins
  • EVP responses documented at the church ruins
