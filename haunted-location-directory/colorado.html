Colorado

22 Locations

Colorado presents one of the strongest mixed-environment investigation profiles in the mountain west, combining historic hotels, mining towns, prison sites, theaters, battle-adjacent landscapes, cemeteries, and alpine wilderness training grounds. Elevation, weather variability, abandoned structures, rail corridors, and strong tourism traffic all create meaningful baseline-control demands across the state.

The Stanley Hotel

Hotel Haunted Historic
Estes Park, CO
Historic Mountain Hotel
Access: Public / Guest Access

Opened in 1909, the Stanley Hotel is Colorado's best-known haunted hotel and one of the most culturally recognized paranormal locations in the United States. Reports have accumulated across guest rooms, concert and ballroom spaces, and underground service areas. The property's age, elevation, and active hospitality use require disciplined baseline control, especially for corridor acoustics and overnight traffic contamination.
