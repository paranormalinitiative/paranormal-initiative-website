Idaho

20 Locations

Idaho combines territorial prisons, mining-town districts, ghost towns, theaters, cemeteries, historic schools, hotels, hot-springs properties, and frontier stations into one of the West’s stronger mixed-environment paranormal reference states. This build prioritizes public-facing locations with persistent folklore, historical weight, and usable site data across Boise, Wallace, Idaho City, Silver City, Albion, Oakley, Soda Springs, Lava Hot Springs, Blackfoot, Pocatello, Cataldo, Hansen, and Donnelly.

Old Idaho Penitentiary

Prison Haunted Historic
Boise, ID
State Historic Site
Access: Public

Territorial prison first constructed in 1870 and operated for more than a century before closing in 1973. Its cell blocks, isolation areas, gallows history, and repeated escape lore make it Idaho’s strongest prison-haunting reference site.

Activity Reported
  • Footsteps and cell-door sounds in empty blocks
  • Shadow figures in the old cell house
  • Presence sensations in solitary and maximum-security areas
  • Cold spots near the women’s ward and administration spaces
  • Apparitional inmate reports from staff and visitors
