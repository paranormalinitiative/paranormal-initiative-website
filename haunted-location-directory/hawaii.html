Hawaii

10 Locations

Hawaii requires a more careful documentation standard than most mainland states. Reported activity at royal, military, religious, and landscape sites often overlaps with living cultural memory, spiritual tradition, and historically traumatic events. This section prioritizes public-facing historic sites and clearly distinguishes between standard haunting reports, battle-related residual patterns, and culturally significant anomaly zones.

ʻIolani Palace

Government Haunted Historic
Honolulu, HI
Royal Palace / Museum
Access: Public

Completed in 1882 as the official royal residence of the Hawaiian Kingdom, ʻIolani Palace remains one of the most historically significant structures in the Pacific. Reports of unusual activity are most often tied to the monarchy period and to the later overthrow-era trauma associated with the imprisonment of Queen Liliʻuokalani. The site should be approached as both a historic landmark and a place of living cultural significance.

Activity Reported
  • Apparition reports associated with royal-era figures
  • Footsteps in upper corridors during quiet periods
  • Presence sensations near private chambers
  • Cold spots in transitional hallway spaces
  • Auditory anomalies reported by staff after hours
Structure Layout
  • Grand Hall and ceremonial rooms
  • Upper royal chambers
  • Imprisonment room associated with Queen Liliʻuokalani
  • Stair halls and perimeter corridors
  • Grounds and barracks-adjacent areas
Location Data

Address: 364 S King St, Honolulu, HI 96813

Website: https://www.iolanipalace.org/

Coordinates: 21.3068° N, 157.8588° W

Classification

Historic royal site with possible residual and intelligent interaction reports. Cultural respect requirements are high; all interpretation should avoid sensational framing.

Nuʻuanu Pali Lookout

Battlefield Haunted Historic
Kāneʻohe, HI
State Wayside / Battlefield
Access: Public

The Nuʻuanu Pali is tied directly to the 1795 Battle of Nuʻuanu, one of the most consequential conflicts in Hawaiian history. Reports from visitors and local tradition describe voices on the wind, sudden temperature shifts, and apparition-like forms in low-visibility conditions. Strong wind conditions, steep terrain, and roadside exposure create significant environmental contamination factors for any field documentation.

Activity Reported
  • Voices or chanting-like sounds carried on extreme winds
  • Apparition reports in cliffside and overlook areas
  • Sudden atmosphere changes and pressure sensations
  • Shadow movement during dusk and low cloud cover
  • Strong residual battle associations in local accounts
Structure Layout
  • Main overlook platform
  • Roadside approach and parking area
  • Cliffside viewing edge
  • Surrounding ridgeline and wind corridor
Location Data

Address: Nuuanu Pali Dr, Kaneohe, HI 96744

Website: https://dlnr.hawaii.gov/dsp/parks/oahu/nuuanu-pali-state-wayside/

Coordinates: 21.3671° N, 157.7931° W

Classification

Residual battlefield imprint / outdoor anomaly zone. Wind and terrain interference are significant and must be documented before interpreting auditory or visual events.

Moana Surfrider

Hotel Haunted Historic
Honolulu, HI
Historic Waikīkī Hotel
Access: Public

Opened in 1901 and widely known as the “First Lady of Waikīkī,” the Moana Surfrider is one of Hawaii’s most frequently cited historic hotel locations for apparition reports. The most persistent accounts center on a woman associated with an early twentieth-century death on the property, with recurring reports of visual anomalies, unexplained room disturbances, and guest-floor activity.

Activity Reported
  • “Lady in white” apparition reports in guest areas
  • Room-specific disturbances and object displacement
  • Footsteps in corridors during overnight hours
  • Cold spots near older wing sections
  • Elevator and hallway anomaly reports
Structure Layout
  • Historic Banyan Wing
  • Tower Wing guest floors
  • Oceanfront lobby and verandas
  • Corridor and elevator zones
  • Beach-facing public spaces
Location Data

Address: 2365 Kalakaua Ave, Honolulu, HI 96815

Website: https://www.marriott.com/en-us/hotels/hnlwi-moana-surfrider-a-westin-resort-and-spa-waikiki-beach/overview/

Coordinates: 21.2765° N, 157.8266° W

Classification

Historic hotel with recurring apparition and room-specific disturbance reports. Active resort environment requires careful control of guest traffic, HVAC, and elevator noise.

Pearl Harbor National Memorial

Military Haunted Historic
Honolulu, HI
National Memorial
Access: Public

Pearl Harbor National Memorial preserves one of the most historically traumatic sites in modern American military history. Reported activity in and around memorial grounds is typically framed in terms of residual wartime imprint rather than conventional haunting language, with visitors and staff describing atmosphere changes, unexplained sounds, and emotional pressure in areas tied closely to the December 7, 1941 attack.

Activity Reported
  • Residual wartime sound reports near memorial zones
  • Atmospheric heaviness and emotional pressure reactions
  • Unexplained voices or distant calls on quieter days
  • Visual anomaly reports near water-facing observation points
  • Staff accounts of after-hours presence sensations
Structure Layout
  • Visitor center and memorial grounds
  • Waterfront observation areas
  • Boat transport route to the USS Arizona Memorial
  • Interpretive and museum spaces
Location Data

Address: 1 Arizona Memorial Place, Honolulu, HI 96818

Website: https://www.nps.gov/perl/

Coordinates: 21.3676° N, 157.9389° W

Classification

Residual military and memorial imprint. Interpretation should remain historically grounded and respectful; the site is foremost a memorial and not an entertainment-oriented investigation location.

USS Arizona Memorial

Military Haunted Historic
Honolulu, HI
War Memorial
Access: Public / Controlled

Built above the remains of the USS Arizona, the memorial marks the resting place of many of the sailors and Marines lost in the 1941 attack. Reports associated with this location are usually described as deeply emotional, atmospheric, and residual in character rather than interactive. Any documentation approach must recognize the memorial’s status as a sacred war grave.

Activity Reported
  • Strong atmosphere changes reported by visitors
  • Unexplained sound impressions over the wreck site
  • Presence sensations during quiet observation periods
  • Emotional and physical response reports disproportionate to conditions
  • Residual wartime association accounts from staff and visitors
Structure Layout
  • Open-air memorial platform
  • Central shrine room
  • Observation openings over the wreck
  • Boat access loading and approach sequence
Location Data

Address: 1 Arizona Memorial Place, Honolulu, HI 96818

Morgan’s Corner

Wilderness Haunted Regional
Honolulu, HI
Roadside / Folklore Zone
Access: Public (Roadside)

Morgan’s Corner is one of Oʻahu’s most persistent modern folklore locations, associated with fatal accidents, homicide lore, and repeated local accounts of oppressive atmosphere, whispers, and apparition-like roadside phenomena. The area is often linked in public retellings both to twentieth-century crime stories and to deeper historical battle narratives in the Nuʻuanu corridor. It should be treated as a folklore-heavy anomaly zone rather than a standard site-based haunting location.

Activity Reported
  • Oppressive atmosphere and sudden fear responses
  • Whispering or voice phenomena near the roadside bend
  • Visual anomaly and shadow-form reports
  • Device drain and equipment irregularity claims
  • Folklore-linked apparitional accounts from local drivers
Structure Layout
  • Hairpin turn / roadside bend
  • Old Pali road corridor
  • Vegetation-lined shoulder areas
  • Nearby trail and ridge approaches
Location Data

Address: Nuuanu Pali Dr, Honolulu, HI 96817

Website: Not publicly maintained

Coordinates: 21.3045° N, 157.8557° W

Classification

Folklore / roadside anomaly zone. Reports are culturally and locally layered, with substantial contamination from modern legend, traffic noise, and expectation effects.

Hawaii’s Plantation Village

Museum Haunted Historic
Waipahu, HI
Historic Outdoor Museum
Access: Public

This reconstructed plantation-era village interprets the multi-ethnic labor history of Hawaii’s sugar industry through relocated and restored worker structures. Reports of unusual activity are usually tied to house interiors, footsteps, and atmosphere changes in preserved dwellings. Because the village is both an educational and cultural site, any paranormal framing should remain secondary to labor history and community memory.

Activity Reported
  • Footsteps in preserved worker houses
  • Presence sensations in specific dwellings
  • Visual anomaly reports in window and doorway zones
  • Unexplained indoor temperature differences
  • Object-shift reports from staff and visitors
Structure Layout
  • Multiple preserved plantation dwellings
  • Outdoor village pathways
  • Cultural exhibit buildings
  • Garden and perimeter zones
Location Data

Address: 94-695 Waipahu St, Waipahu, HI 96797

Website: https://hawaiiplantationvillage.org/

Coordinates: 21.3854° N, 158.0119° W

Classification

Historic cultural site with possible residual occupational imprint. Environmental interpretation should remain subordinate to plantation labor history and community context.

King Kamehameha V Judiciary History Center

Government Haunted Historic
Honolulu, HI
Aliʻiōlani Hale / Museum
Access: Public

Located inside Aliʻiōlani Hale, one of Honolulu’s most historically significant civic buildings, the Judiciary History Center interprets Hawaiian legal and governmental history. Reports tied to the broader building include unusual sounds, footsteps, and after-hours atmosphere changes in courtroom-adjacent areas. As with other civic-monarchy crossover sites in Honolulu, the building carries layered governmental and cultural significance that should temper any investigative framing.

Activity Reported
  • Footsteps in upper and adjacent government corridors
  • After-hours voice reports in quiet rooms
  • Presence sensations in courtroom-related areas
  • Door and corridor anomalies during low-traffic periods
  • Localized cold spots in older interior sections
Structure Layout
  • First-floor museum exhibits
  • Aliʻiōlani Hale corridors
  • Courtroom-adjacent areas
  • Stair halls and entry sequence

Kawaiahaʻo Church

Church Haunted Historic
Honolulu, HI
Historic Church
Access: Public / Worship Site

Often called the “Westminster Abbey of Hawaiʻi,” Kawaiahaʻo Church has deep ties to Hawaiian monarchy history, royal ceremonies, and generations of burial and memorial practice. Reports of unusual activity at and around the church are generally subtle, centering on footsteps, visual impressions, and atmosphere changes within a site already saturated with spiritual meaning. This is a living church first, and any TPI documentation should remain culturally respectful and non-intrusive.

Activity Reported
  • Footstep reports in quiet sanctuary periods
  • Presence sensations near burial and memorial-associated areas
  • Visual anomaly reports in pew and aisle lines
  • Atmospheric changes during off-hours access
  • Subtle voice impressions reported by staff and visitors
Structure Layout
  • Main sanctuary
  • Aisles and pew rows
  • Perimeter burial and memorial grounds
  • Entry and side access points
Location Data

Address: 957 Punchbowl St, Honolulu, HI 96813

Website: https://kawaiahaochurch.com/

Coordinates: 21.3045° N, 157.8576° W

Hilton Hawaiian Village

Hotel Haunted Historic
Honolulu, HI
Resort Complex
Access: Public

The Hilton Hawaiian Village occupies a large Waikīkī coastal footprint and has accumulated folklore and witness accounts associated with older land use, burial-ground sensitivity, and unexplained activity within certain towers and corridors. Because the property is a large active resort complex, environmental contamination from heavy guest traffic, elevators, ocean noise, and commercial operations is substantial. It is best treated as a mixed-use hospitality site with localized reports rather than a single concentrated haunting location.

Activity Reported
  • Room-specific anomaly reports from guests
  • Corridor footsteps and voice impressions
  • Presence sensations in older tower areas
  • Localized cold spots despite active HVAC conditions
  • Folklore-linked burial-ground associations in public retellings
Structure Layout
  • Multiple guest towers
  • Interior corridors and elevator zones
  • Lagoon and beachfront public areas
  • Lobby, retail, and event spaces
Location Data

Address: 2005 Kalia Rd, Honolulu, HI 96815

Website: https://www.hilton.com/en/hotels/hnlhvhh-hilton-hawaiian-village-waikiki-beach-resort/

Coordinates: 21.2827° N, 157.8388° W

Classification

Localized hospitality-site disturbance reports within a high-noise resort environment. Investigation value is limited unless narrowed to a specific tower, floor, or repeating room cluster.
