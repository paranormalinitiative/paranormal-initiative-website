Methodology

Investigation begins before the lights go out.

Responsible paranormal investigation depends on preparation, observation, documentation, environmental context, and careful reporting. The goal is not to force a paranormal conclusion, but to understand what happened as honestly as possible.

01

Intake & Case Context

Document the client account, reported activity, dates, patterns, people present, property details, and known stressors before the investigation begins.

02

Baseline Observation

Record ordinary conditions first: sound, airflow, lighting, temperature, EMF, structure noise, appliance cycles, nearby roads, weather, and human movement.

03

Equipment Documentation

Log what tools were used, where they were placed, when they were active, who operated them, and what limitations each tool has.

04

Evidence Handling

Preserve originals, keep notes attached to time and location, avoid destructive edits, and review media with natural explanations in mind.

05

Ethical Reporting

Communicate responsibly. Distinguish confirmed facts, witness claims, possible explanations, unresolved material, and speculation.

Methodology Framework

Observation Before Interpretation

Write what happened before deciding what it means. A knock, shadow, voice-like sound, or cold area is first an observation.

Documentation Before Conclusion

Time, location, witness account, equipment settings, weather, and team movement turn memory into a reviewable case record.

Ethics Before Excitement

Permission, privacy, emotional safety, property care, and careful language matter more than dramatic evidence claims.

Field Note Format

A useful field note can be short: time, location, observation, context, and follow-up. Example: "9:42 PM, second-floor hallway. Three knocks heard by two investigators. HVAC off. No team movement logged. Exterior traffic audible. Marked for audio/video comparison."
