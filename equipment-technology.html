Field Technology

Equipment is useful when its limitations are documented as carefully as its readings.

Investigation tools can help observe conditions, preserve media, and organize a session. They do not prove paranormal activity on their own. The value comes from context, baselines, repeatability, and honest review.

Environmental Sensors

Temperature, humidity, barometric pressure, airflow, lightning, solar, lunar, and weather context.

Audio Recorders

EVP sessions, ambient noise baselines, microphone placement, gain settings, and contamination tracking.

Cameras & Video

Still photos, video, night vision, frame review, compression artifacts, light behavior, and original preservation.

EMF & K2 Meters

Electrical field observations, baseline comparison, nearby wiring, appliance cycles, and device limitations.

Thermal Tools

Temperature contrast, drafts, reflective surfaces, camera calibration, and ordinary heat sources.

Spirit Boxes & Experimental Devices

Radio sweep tools, ITC experiments, ACS, water ITC, steam ITC, and research-first documentation.
